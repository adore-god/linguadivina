A Psychological Reading of Scripture

John: Abiding Love

In a world brimming with uncertainty, one force stands unshakeable: love. Not only love as a feeling or fleeting romance, but love as the unifying thread that connects YHVH/LORD to the court of power of Elohim.

John 15:8 reveals a truth about this connection and its power to manifest the assumed state:

By this my Father is glorified, that you bear much fruit and so prove to be my disciples.

Here, bearing fruit represents the external evidence of YHVH/LORD fully assuming the Ehyeh/I AM. The "Father" symbolises the awareness of being, and to glorify Him is to manifest the assumed identity effortlessly, with Elohim enforcing the realisation of that state.

The Essence of Abiding: Living in Assumed Love

To abide in love is for YHVH/LORD to dwell in the unwavering assumption of Ehyeh/I AM — already fulfilled and aligned with Elohim. John 15:9–10 emphasises this union:

As the Father has loved me, so have I loved you. Abide in my love.

Abiding is the deliberate act of YHVH/LORD assuming Ehyeh/I AM fully. When this is done, Elohim enforces the manifestation in the outer world. "The Father" represents the "I AM", sustaining the assumed state (Jesus), inviting YHVH/LORD to remain in that same assumed consciousness.

This mirrors the symbolic union in Genesis 2:24:

Therefore shall a man leave his father and his mother, and shall cleave unto his wife: and they shall be one flesh.

Here, YHVH/LORD leaves previous limitations ("father and mother") and cleaves to Ehyeh/I AM ("wife"), forming one unified state. Elohim ensures the continuity and enforcement of this union.

Love as the Source of Manifestation: Bearing the Wish Fulfilled

Alignment with love equates to alignment with Elohim’s enforcement. Love is the fertile soil from which the Ehyeh/I AM grows, producing fruit — the visible evidence of the assumed identity.

These things I have spoken to you, that my joy may be in you, and that your joy may be full.

Joy is the external expression of YHVH/LORD dwelling in Ehyeh/I AM. Persisting in love ensures Elohim enforces the outer reflection of this inner assumption. Success, harmony, and abundance are fruits borne from sustained union of consciousness and assumed identity.

Neville Goddard teaches that imagination is the creative engine, and love is its highest vibration. When YHVH/LORD imagines from love, Ehyeh/I AM becomes real within consciousness, and Elohim manifests it externally. This is the mystical "one flesh" of Genesis 2:24.

Faith and Love: The Divine Bond Through Assumption

Faith, unified with love, becomes unstoppable in creative enforcement. John 15:13–14 highlights this:

Greater love has no one than this, that someone lay down his life for his friends. You are my friends if you do what I command you.

To "lay down one’s life" is for YHVH/LORD to relinquish old consciousness, fully inhabiting Ehyeh/I AM. Elohim enforces this assumed state, ensuring it manifests. Faith grounded in love guarantees the reality of the assumed identity becomes tangible.

Living in Love: The Constant Assumption of Abundance

Living in love is living perpetually in the assumption of abundance. YHVH/LORD remains unmoved by appearances, trusting Elohim to enforce the assumed state.

These things I have spoken to you, that in me you may have peace. In the world you will have tribulation. But take heart; I have overcome the world.

Peace is the fruit of abiding in love. Persistent assumption allows YHVH/LORD to direct circumstances, with Elohim enforcing the outcomes consistent with Ehyeh/I AM.

Conclusion: The Fruit of Love and Assumption

Abiding in love is assuming the highest state of being. Accepting perpetual love invites Elohim to manifest all that aligns with Ehyeh/I AM.

I have chosen you and appointed you so that you might go and bear fruit—fruit that will last.

The enduring fruit is the embodiment of the assumed identity. The union of YHVH/LORD and Ehyeh/I AM—symbolically "one flesh"—becomes the creative act through which desires manifest under Elohim's enforcement.
