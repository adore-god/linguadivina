A Psychological Reading of Scripture

Neville Goddard: Crown of Thorns Summary

In Neville Goddard’s interpretation of the Bible, the Crown of Thorns is not just a physical object worn by Jesus during the crucifixion, but a symbolic representation of the limitations and burdens of the human mind. It is a deeply profound metaphysical image that speaks to the way the mind constrains itself through beliefs and assumptions.

The Crown of Thorns: A Metaphysical Symbol

The crown is often associated with authority, power, and dominion. In the Bible, when Jesus is crowned with thorns, it reflects the conscious mind's struggle and the limitations that we place on ourselves through negative thinking, doubt, and fear.

The Thorns: Limiting Beliefs and Assumptions

Thorns are often seen in nature as something that pricks or wounds—they represent the mental and emotional pain that comes from negative thoughts, limiting beliefs, and false assumptions. In Neville Goddard’s framework, thorns symbolize the consciousness that is not yet awakened or is still bound by the old patterns of thinking.

  • The thorns represent the crown of limitations: These are the self-imposed restrictions on your ability to experience abundance, love, health, and joy. The crown signifies that the mind, in its unenlightened state, rules over the person, but does so by limiting them, keeping them in a state of suffering or lack.

  • The crown of thorns represents mental suffering that comes from identifying with the false self or the ego. This false identity consists of negative beliefs about who you are, what you deserve, and what is possible in your life.

Jesus and the Crown of Thorns: The Awakening of Consciousness

In Neville Goddard’s teaching, Jesus represents the divine imagination or I AM consciousness that exists within every individual. The crown of thorns, placed upon Jesus, is a powerful symbol of the conscious mind before it has fully recognized its divine nature.

  • The crown of thorns signifies the suffering caused by limited thinking. Just as Jesus was mocked by the soldiers who placed the thorns upon him, we often mock ourselves through self-doubt, limiting beliefs, and negative assumptions about our own potential.

  • Jesus, however, embraces this crown of thorns, transcends the limitations, and overcomes the false self. This act symbolizes the transformation of the human mind from the old, limited, suffering state to the higher consciousness that knows its divine creative power.

Through the crucifixion (the final transformation), Jesus frees the mind from its false assumptions and represents the awakening of consciousness to its true power to create. In Neville Goddard’s interpretation, the crown of thorns is the symbolic representation of the struggle that every individual faces in awakening their imagination and overcoming the false beliefs that have held them back.

The Crown of Thorns as the Struggle of Consciousness

  1. Mental Pain: The crown, being made of thorns, represents the mental pain that results from believing in limiting beliefs—ideas like “I can’t,” “I’m not worthy,” or “I don’t deserve.” These thoughts crown the mind with suffering because they are rooted in the ego, which creates separation and limits.

  2. Transformation of Thought: Just as Jesus endured the crown of thorns, he shows us how to transcend the limitations of the human mind. The thorns are ultimately removed when we stop identifying with the false, limited beliefs and recognize our true identity as divine creators.

The Key to Freedom: Removing the Thorns

The process of removing the crown of thorns is about clearing your mind of false assumptions and identifying with your true self—the I AM that is capable of creating reality. When you stop identifying with the pain, doubt, and fear, you can wear a crown of glory, signifying your mastery over the mental realm and your divine power to shape reality.

The crown of thorns ultimately teaches us that suffering is the result of identifying with false beliefs and limiting thoughts. The path to freedom is about awakening to the truth of your divine power and removing the thorns of mental limitations.

In Summary: The Crown of Thorns and Neville Goddard’s Teachings

  • The crown of thorns represents the limitations of the mind—the false beliefs and negative assumptions that create suffering.

  • Jesus represents the I AM consciousness that exists in everyone, and his crown of thorns represents the struggle of consciousness to overcome the false self and awaken to its divine creative power.

  • Removing the thorns is about transcending limiting beliefs and accepting your true identity as a creator.

  • Ultimately, the crown of thorns is a symbol of mental suffering caused by false beliefs, and through recognizing your divine imagination, you can transform suffering into creative power.

By removing the crown of thorns, you embrace the divine mind, and in doing so, you can create a life free from limitation.
