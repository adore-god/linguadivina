A Psychological Reading of Scripture

Touching Jesus and the Flow of Blood


The Flow Stops: Neville Goddard on the Power of Belief and Healing

Neville Goddard, a master of metaphysical interpretation, offers a profound perspective on the biblical story of the woman with the issue of blood (Mark 5, Luke 8, Matthew 9). He presents this event not as a mere historical tale, but as a powerful allegory for the influence of our inner world on shaping our outer reality.

The Unceasing Drain: A Symbol of Persistent Problems

For Neville, the woman’s continuous flow of blood is not just a physical ailment. It represents:

  • Chronic, unwanted conditions that drain our vitality.

  • This could be health issues, financial struggles, relationship problems, or any aspect of life that feels persistently draining and resistant to change.

These issues leave us feeling depleted and hopeless, mirroring the woman’s ongoing suffering.

The Decisive Touch: A Shift in Consciousness

The woman’s act of reaching out to touch the hem of Jesus’ garment is not just a physical interaction — it is a shift in consciousness.

  • The "hem of the garment" represents the edge of Jesus’ realized state of wholeness — a symbol of healing and well-being.

  • Her touch signifies a mental and emotional connection to this desired state, marking a pivotal change in her mindset.

Belief: The Engine of Transformation

At the core of Neville's interpretation is the woman's unwavering belief that the single act of touching Jesus' garment would bring healing.

  • Belief is the active force that triggers change.

  • It wasn't the physical touch itself, but the inner knowing and certainty that preceded and accompanied the action that held the transformative power.

The Outflow of Power: Manifestation in the External

When Jesus felt power leave him, it symbolized the tangible manifestation of the woman’s belief in her external reality.

  • Her internal shift in consciousness resulted in a corresponding physical change in her condition.

  • The unseen power of conviction transformed into a visible result.

Faith Made Whole: The Source of All Healing

Jesus' statement, "Your faith has made you whole," underscores a crucial principle:

  • The healing didn’t come from an external source but from the woman’s own faith.

  • Her unwavering acceptance and conviction in the reality of her desired state initiated the transformation.

This highlights the innate power within each individual to shape their own experiences.

Key Takeaways from Neville's Interpretation:

  • Consciousness Creates: Your dominant beliefs and feelings actively shape your external world.

  • Persistent Problems Reflect Inner States: Unwanted conditions are often sustained by persistent negative thought patterns and assumptions.

  • Decisive Belief Triggers Change: A firm, unwavering conviction in your desired outcome is essential for transformation.

  • The Power Within: The ability to heal and create your reality lies within your own consciousness.

In Conclusion:

Neville uses this compelling biblical narrative to teach us that by decisively shifting our inner beliefs and fully assuming the feeling of our wish fulfilled, we can stop the "flow" of any unwanted circumstance and step into a state of wholeness and well-being.

The power to change our lives resides within us.
