A Psychological Reading of Scripture

Placed in the Rock: The Skull, the Passing, and the Back Parts of God

Exodus 33 contains one of the most precisely encoded passages in all of Scripture. Moses asks to see the glory of God, and what follows is not a historical spectacle but a sequential map of how consciousness discovers its own nature through assumed identity. The passage reads:

And the Lord said, Behold, there is a place by me, and you will be put in a gap of the rock, covered by my hand till I have gone past. Then my hand will be taken away and you will see my back: but my face may not be seen.
Exodus 33:21–23

Every element carries precise operational weight within the mechanics of YHVH/LORD, Ehyeh/I AM, and Elohim. The rock, the cleft, the covering hand, the passing, and the back parts are not decorative detail. They describe, in sequence, how present consciousness is positioned, sheltered, and finally shown the evidence of what has been enforced.

Moses: The State of Desire

Within the framework of names as identity codes, the name Moses discloses the nature of the state he encodes. Drawn from the Egyptian root meaning to draw out or to be born, Moses is YHVH/LORD in the condition of pressing toward a truth not yet fully occupied. He is present consciousness stirring toward a deeper assumed identity, the desire to know without yet having arrived at knowing.

This is the first movement in the Ask, Believe, Receive sequence. Moses asking to see God's glory is YHVH/LORD recognising the desire within consciousness. The request itself is the Ask. What follows in the rock, the hand, and the passing is the structured mechanics of the Believe and the Receive.

It is significant that Moses never enters the Promised Land. That crossing belongs to Joshua, whose name is the Hebrew form of Jesus, meaning YHVH is salvation or YHVH delivers. The name Joshua encodes the state of fulfilment itself. Moses is the desire; Joshua is the embodied I AM that crosses into the realised condition. The Promised Land is not geography but the fully enforced identity, the state in which YHVH/LORD has assumed Ehyeh/I AM completely and Elohim has made it manifest.

The Rock: The Skull as the Seat of Consciousness

God places Moses in the cleft of a rock. This is not a geological location. The rock in this passage is the human skull, the cranium, the physical seat of imagination and the organ through which all assumed identity is generated and projected. Neville Goddard was precise on this point: the rock is your own skull, the place of the skull, Golgotha.

Golgotha, the hill on which the crucifixion takes place in the Gospel accounts, means the place of the skull in Aramaic. That identification is not coincidental. The cross, the death of the old state, occurs at the place of the skull because all transformation of identity originates within the cranium. What happens at Golgotha in the Gospel is the same mechanics operating in Exodus 33: an old assumed identity is vacated so that a new I AM can be enforced by Elohim.

And when they had come to a place called Golgotha, that is to say, The place of a skull.
Matthew 27:33

To be placed in the cleft of the rock is to be positioned within the skull itself, inside the organ of imagination, held in the creative seat from which all assumed identity radiates outward into enforced reality. YHVH/LORD is not standing beside a boulder in the Sinai. Present consciousness is being positioned within the very structure through which Elohim operates. The cleft is the interior space of the cranium where the new I AM is being formed before it is enforced.

David's declaration in the Psalms uses the same encoding:

He is my rock and my salvation, my high tower; I will not be moved.
Psalm 62:6

The rock as unshakeable foundation is the skull as unshakeable seat of consciousness. The high tower is the elevated perspective of the assumed I AM. The refusal to be moved is Elohim enforcing the ruling identity against all contrary evidence from the senses.

The Hand: The Veil Held Over Present Perception

While positioned in the cleft, God covers Moses with His hand. The hand in Scripture consistently encodes directed authority, the operative force of Elohim working through YHVH/LORD. Here it also functions as a specific veil. Present consciousness, while still occupying its current assumed state, cannot perceive the enforcement of the new identity as it occurs. The covering is not punishment. It is the necessary structure of the transition itself.

This is precisely the mechanics of the leave and cleave principle. YHVH/LORD must leave the familiar state before Elohim can enforce the new one. During that transitional position, the cranium is held in the cleft, the hand covers, and the old perception is suspended. The bridegroom has left the father's house but has not yet seen the bride unveiled. The covering hand holds consciousness in the gap between the vacated identity and the arrived one.

Neville described this as the veil of sense-consciousness, the condition of remaining clothed in the present assumed state while the new I AM is being established. You cannot perceive God's face, the full unveiled enforcement of your assumed identity, while YHVH/LORD is still presenting the old filing to Elohim. The hand remains until the passing is complete.

The Passing: Elohim Enforcing the Assumed Identity

God passes by while Moses is held in the rock. This passing is Elohim executing the enforcement of the assumed I AM. It is the moment in the seed narrative when germination occurs underground, entirely invisible to the observer above the soil. It is Joseph in the pit at the precise moment the statutes of reality begin shifting toward the palace. It is the still small voice in 1 Kings 19 that follows the wind, the earthquake, and the fire, none of which carry the enforcement, because YHVH/LORD is not in the dramatic external phenomena but in the quiet interior assumption.

And after the fire, a still small voice.
1 Kings 19:12

Elijah at the same mountain, receiving the same passing of YHVH/LORD, learns that the enforcement does not announce itself through outward spectacle. The passing is silent. The Judges and Rulers of I AM operate through the laws of creation without theatre. The covering hand is still in place during the passing because Elohim enforces without requiring the observer's permission or perception.

The Back Parts: Manifestation as Retrospective Evidence

When the hand is removed, Moses sees God's back. He does not see the face. Within the mechanics of the key, this is the precise description of how Elohim's enforcement is perceived within time. YHVH/LORD does not witness the moment of enforcement as it is occurring. What is visible is always the aftermath: the outer world rearranged in alignment with what was assumed inwardly as true.

Neville described this accurately: you do not see God's face, you see His back, meaning you see the outer world as evidence of what you accepted inwardly as the ruling I AM. The back parts are not a diminished revelation. They are the only form in which the operation of Elohim becomes visible to consciousness still operating within time. The vine produces fruit that testifies, after the fact, to what was latent in the branch. The shoot emerging from the earth shows what happened in the dark. Joseph emerges into the palace and finds that the statutes have already moved in his favour.

And he said, So is the kingdom of God, as if a man should cast seed into the ground; And should sleep, and rise night and day, and the seed should spring and grow up, he knows not how.
Mark 4:26–27

The man who sows the seed sees only the back parts. He does not witness the germination. He rises and sleeps and rises again, and the enforcement has already occurred by the time the shoot is visible. This is Moses seeing God's back after the hand is lifted. The passing has completed. Elohim has enforced. What remains is the evidence in the outer world of what was assumed within the skull.

The Face Withheld: The Fully Unveiled I AM

God's face cannot be seen by Moses in his present state. The face of God, within the mechanics of the key, is the fully unveiled enforcement of the assumed I AM, the condition in which YHVH/LORD has completely occupied Ehyeh/I AM and Elohim enforces without remainder or veil. This is the state Joshua encodes in his name. It is the Promised Land as a condition of consciousness, not a coordinate on a map.

Paul encodes the same distinction:

For now we see through a glass, darkly; but then face to face: now I know in part; but then shall I know even as also I am known.
1 Corinthians 13:12

The glass is the current veil of perception, identical to the covering hand in Exodus 33. The face to face is the state in which the old filing has been fully vacated, the new I AM fully assumed, and Elohim enforces the identity without the partial knowing that characterises the transitional position. Moses occupies the glass. Joshua crosses through it. The back parts are what Moses sees. The face is what Joshua enters.

The Name Declared in the Passing

Immediately after the passing, as the back parts are seen, the Name is declared:

And the Lord went past before him, saying, The Lord, the Lord God, full of pity and grace, slow to wrath and great in mercy and faith.
Exodus 34:6

This is not a description of divine personality. It is the disclosure of the nature of the operational Name as it enforces: YHVH/LORD Elohim, the Judges and Rulers of I AM, whose character extends mercy across generations and maintains the covenant of assumed identity. The Name declared in the passing is the same Name given in Exodus 3:14: Ehyeh Asher Ehyeh, I AM that I AM. Elohim enforces always and only what YHVH/LORD has assumed as the ruling I AM. The declaration confirms the mechanics that have just been demonstrated in the rock, the hand, and the passing.

Exodus 33 is therefore a sequential diagram of the entire engine, operating inside the skull. YHVH/LORD in the present state is placed within the cranium, the cleft of the rock. The covering hand suspends present perception during the enforcement. Elohim passes, executing the statutes of creation in favour of the assumed identity. The hand is removed. The back parts are seen: the outer world rearranged as evidence of what was assumed in the dark. The face remains the destination, the fully realised state of the Promised Land, the condition in which the jurisdictional error is corrected, the new filing is accepted, and Elohim enforces the ruling I AM into the full substance of lived experience.
