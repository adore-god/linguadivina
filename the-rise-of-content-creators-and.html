The Rise of Content Creators and Influencers: Parallel to Neville Goddard’s States of Being

In today’s digital age, content creators and influencers have become pivotal figures in shaping the way we view ourselves, our desires, and our reality. With platforms like Instagram, YouTube, and TikTok, individuals are not only able to showcase their lives but are also becoming powerful forces in creating trends, shaping opinions, and driving cultural shifts. But what’s fascinating is how this phenomenon mirrors Neville Goddard’s philosophies on states of being and the creative power of consciousness.

Neville Goddard, known for his teachings on manifestation, taught that consciousness creates reality, and that by shifting our state of being—our internal assumptions, beliefs, and imaginations—we can manifest the world around us. This idea can be beautifully aligned with the rise of content creators and influencers, who are, in many ways, demonstrating how to influence and create reality through their perception of self and the imagined lives they share with their audiences.

The Power of States of Being

Neville Goddard’s teachings suggest that our external reality is simply a reflection of our internal state. The state of being we inhabit determines the experiences we attract. This concept is central to Neville’s philosophy: by assuming the feeling of the wish fulfilled—whether it’s wealth, love, success, or anything else—we align ourselves with that state, and the universe mirrors it back to us.

In this light, influencers are living embodiments of this very principle. They project a particular state of being—whether it’s someone full of confidence, creativity, abundance, or happiness—and in doing so, they create a narrative or reality that resonates with their followers. By stepping into their chosen state, they attract opportunities, collaborations, and followers who are aligned with their energy and the reality they project.

For example, a fashion influencer assumes the state of someone who is fashionable and successful. They embody confidence in their style and are perceived as a trendsetter, which then leads to more followers, brand deals, and the manifestation of greater opportunities. The influencer is assuming a state of being—and through this assumption, they manifest the reality that aligns with that state.

The Role of Imagination in Content Creation

At the core of Neville Goddard’s teachings is the idea of imagination as a creative tool. He believed that the imagination was the most powerful force in shaping our lives. What we imagine and truly feel ourselves to be is what we bring into existence. The imagination is not merely a tool for fantasy, but for the creation of reality itself.

Content creators do this constantly. When they create content, they are projecting a version of their imagination into the world. They imagine how they want to be seen, what they want to project, and how they want to inspire others. Whether through storytelling, visuals, or curated experiences, they channel their inner world into tangible forms that influence how others perceive them. Through this process, they actively participate in the co-creation of their reality.

Think about it: an influencer imagines themselves as a leader in a certain niche. They visualise and feel that they are already at the top of their field, attracting brands, followers, and recognition. Their content aligns with this vision, and as they continue to embody this state, they attract the corresponding external reality—a growing audience, lucrative collaborations, and more influence.

The Influence of Perception: How Content Creators Shape Reality

One of the key principles in Neville Goddard's teachings is that perception shapes reality. What we believe and perceive ourselves to be is reflected in the world around us. In the digital age, content creators wield immense power over the perceptions of their audiences. They craft the narratives of who they are, what they stand for, and how they want to be seen, and this projected image shapes the experiences they have.

The audience plays a crucial role in this dynamic as well. Just as Neville teaches that our reality is shaped by our assumptions, influencers also rely on their audiences’ assumptions about them. When a large group of followers assumes that an influencer is authentic, successful, or relatable, those perceptions become part of the influencer’s state of being, further enhancing their reality.

For example, if an influencer is able to inspire a sense of trust, confidence, and relatability, their followers will begin to project these qualities onto them, reinforcing the influencer’s projected state of being. Over time, the influencer becomes identified with these traits—whether or not they consciously realise it—and this identification becomes a self-fulfilling prophecy, manifesting greater success and influence.

Influencers as Manifestation Guides

In a way, influencers today can be seen as modern-day manifestation guides, teaching through their own lived experiences how states of being shape our reality. Just as Neville Goddard suggested, they embody a particular state, broadcast it through their content, and watch as the world responds.

But unlike traditional spiritual teachers, influencers often do this in a very public and relatable way. They show their audiences the power of embodying a certain mindset, whether it’s about confidence, success, or personal growth. By following them, viewers are not just watching entertainment—they are witnessing the manifestation process in real time. They see how assumptions and inner states reflect in the external world, offering an accessible example of Neville’s teachings in action.

Interestingly, the concept of manifestation is not just confined to spiritual teachers. Prominent figures like Jim Carrey and Oprah Winfrey have spoken openly about the role of manifestation in their own lives, underscoring the widespread belief in the power of consciousness to shape reality.

  • Jim Carrey famously shared how he visualised and wrote a cheque to himself for $10 million while struggling in his early career. He carried it in his wallet for years, embodying the state of being a successful actor. Later, he received a movie role that paid him exactly $10 million, showcasing the power of imagination and assumption in manifesting his dreams.

  • Similarly, Oprah Winfrey has frequently spoken about the role of manifestation in her success. She has shared how, through her show, she constantly visualised herself achieving great things, including becoming one of the most influential figures in the world. Oprah attributes much of her success to the belief in her ability to create her own reality, demonstrating the profound impact that states of being and self-belief can have on one’s external life.

Both of these figures have exemplified Neville Goddard’s idea that what we imagine and believe we can achieve becomes our reality, further cementing the idea that consciousness creates our experiences.

Conclusion: Content Creation as a Reflection of Consciousness

The rise of content creators and influencers reflects the power of states of being in today’s world. Just as Neville Goddard taught that our assumptions shape our reality, influencers are showing us how projecting a certain state of being—through imagination, confidence, and consistency—can manifest in tangible results. The very act of creating content becomes an exercise in conscious creation, where individuals align their inner state with the world they wish to manifest.

In this sense, content creators and influencers are not just sharing their lives—they are engaging in a deliberate practice of manifestation, modelling the creative power of consciousness for their audiences. Whether they are aware of it or not, they are living proof of Neville Goddard’s teachings: the world responds to the state of being we project, and by assuming the reality we desire, we can shape the world around us.
