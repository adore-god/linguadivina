A Psychological Reading of Scripture

Jesus: The New Risen Self

When we explore the Bible through Neville Goddard’s teachings and the Law of Assumption, we see that it is not merely a book of moral commandments but a detailed map of consciousness. Among all the events described in scripture, none is more pivotal than Jesus’s resurrection. While much attention is given to his crucifixion, it is his rising — his resurrection — that demonstrates the operational power of assumed identity enforced by Elohim.

Fixing The Idea: The Necessary Crucifixion

The crucifixion symbolises YHVH/LORD fully occupying a new Ehyeh/I AM while letting the old consciousness fall away. It represents the psychological act of "nailing" the assumed identity within the presence of Elohim, who must now enforce this state. The crucifixion is the moment YHVH/LORD detaches from prior identity and submits the new I AM to the statutes of consciousness.

Yet, this act alone does not complete creation. True creative power unfolds in the subsequent rising, the moment when Elohim manifests the assumed identity in reality.

Rising As The Triumph Of Assumption

The resurrection illustrates YHVH/LORD fully living as Ehyeh/I AM, with Elohim enforcing the reality of this state. The new self is no longer a conceptual idea but a fully operative identity, guiding thought and action.

Jesus is no longer among the dead; he has risen, demonstrating the new assumed state fully realised. (Luke 24:5–6)
The rising occurs as previously declared by YHVH/LORD, showing that Elohim enforces the assumed identity. (Matthew 28:6)

Appearing in Closed Rooms: Beyond Limitation

After the resurrection, Jesus appears in confined spaces, demonstrating that when YHVH/LORD fully occupies Ehyeh/I AM, Elohim ensures manifestation regardless of perceived obstacles. Locked doors symbolise limitations, yet the risen identity moves effortlessly through them, transcending conditions that once constrained consciousness.

Jesus appears among the disciples even though the doors are locked, symbolising that the new state cannot be obstructed. (John 20:19)
He appears to Thomas, showing that even doubtful aspects of consciousness must acknowledge the risen identity. (John 20:26)

Ascending Into Your New Identity

Jesus’ ascension represents the final act of YHVH/LORD fully dwelling as Ehyeh/I AM, with Elohim permanently enforcing this state. The new identity becomes the default reality, no longer temporary but fully integrated into consciousness.

Jesus departs from the disciples and is carried up, confirming the permanent dwelling of the new state within consciousness. (Luke 24:51)

Living In The End: The True Resurrection

Neville taught that the Law of Assumption is about living in the end — feeling from the fulfilled desire. The resurrection symbolises this principle:

  • You accept the new state (crucifixion).
  • You rise into it, living from it (resurrection).
  • You ascend, dwelling in it permanently (ascension).

Conclusion: The Triumph Of "I Am"

The story of Jesus’s resurrection invites us to move beyond perpetual "fixing" and into the joy of embodiment. It calls us to stop returning to old mental patterns and instead to walk in the light of the new state, moving confidently as if the wish were already fulfilled. In Neville’s words, "Dare to assume you are what you want to be, and you will compel conditions to conform to your assumption." The resurrection is the boldest invitation to do exactly that — to rise now.
