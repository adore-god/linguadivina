A Psychological Reading of Scripture

Nephilim Meaning: The Fallen Ones and the Jurisdictional Error in Genesis 6

The Bible, read through the linguistic engine of YHVH/LORD, Ehyeh/I AM, and Elohim, discloses how identity shapes lived experience. The creation story opens with this structure already in motion.

And the earth was waste and without form; and it was dark on the face of the deep: and the Spirit of God was moving on the face of the waters. Genesis 1:2

YHVH/LORD, present consciousness, moves across formless potential. Genesis 1:11 then decrees that every seed reproduces after its own kind, Elohim as Judges and Rulers establishing the governing law of identity within creation. The full creative declaration arrives near the end of that first chapter.

And God said, Let us make man in our image, like us: and let him have rule over the fish of the sea and over the birds of the air and over the cattle and over all the earth and over every living thing which goes flat on the earth. Genesis 1:26

The plural voice of Elohim establishes that identity is the primary creative unit, the point from which all manifestation proceeds. This is the blueprint for human consciousness: YHVH/LORD assumes an Ehyeh/I AM and Elohim is bound to enforce it after its kind. Every subsequent narrative in Scripture demonstrates that mechanism in motion. Which brings the reading to one of the strangest passages in Genesis: the account of the Nephilim.

The Men of Great Name

Genesis 6 opens with YHVH/LORD spreading across the face of the earth and a particular union taking place within that expansion.

There were men of great strength and size on the earth in those days; and after that, when the sons of God had connection with the daughters of men, they gave birth to children: these were the great men of old days, the men of great name. Genesis 6:4

Within the framework of the key, this verse encodes the jurisdictional mechanics of identity. The sons of God represent the governing I AM, the declaration of identity that YHVH/LORD presents to Elohim as its Ehyeh. The daughters of men represent the perceptual field: what YHVH/LORD receives through the senses, the evidence of outward circumstances, the voice of appearances rather than the voice of a chosen truth. When the governing I AM descends into union with sensory data rather than with a consciously chosen state, Elohim enforces the offspring of that union. The result is men of great name, towering identities rooted not in a chosen Ehyeh but in the amplification of external appearance. These are the Nephilim.

The Hebrew root of Nephilim points to falling, and the word carries two shades that the key illuminates equally. As a simple plural, Nephilim means the fallen ones: the product of YHVH/LORD collapsing into what appears before it and constructing an identity from that collapse. In its causative form the meaning shifts to those who cause others to fall, which is precisely what the grasshopper episode in Numbers demonstrates. The giants do not merely stand there. Their presence causes YHVH/LORD in the ten spies to fall into a diminished I AM, filing "grasshopper" with Elohim and receiving grasshopper conditions in return. The Nephilim are therefore both fallen and falling: fallen in origin because they were generated by a false filing, and active in their effect because a towering false identity causes the consciousness that encounters it to contract around a lesser Ehyeh.

When All Thoughts of the Heart Were Evil

The verse that follows the Nephilim account names the condition from which they arise.

And the Lord saw that the sin of man was great on the earth, and that all the thoughts of his heart were evil. Genesis 6:5

Sin here carries the sense of missing the mark. Every thought of the heart is filing the wrong claim with Elohim. The governing structure has no option but to rule accordingly. The flood that follows is not arbitrary destruction but the dissolution of a reality structure entirely misaligned with the blueprint of Genesis 1:26. The old formation must be cleared so that the engine can begin again from a true Ehyeh/I AM assumed and held. Noah, who walks in alignment with the governing structure, carries the seed of the new order through that dissolution. The reversal is identical in kind to what the key identifies in the account of Joseph: the pit yields to the palace when YHVH/LORD assumes the ruling identity and Elohim enforces it.

Grasshoppers in the Promised Land

The Nephilim reappear when twelve spies enter Canaan. Ten of them return with a report shaped entirely by what they have seen rather than by the identity they have been given.

and there have we seen giants -- the sons of Anak are of the giants -- and we were in our sight as grasshoppers, and so we were also in their sight. Numbers 13:33

This is the mechanism made visible. YHVH/LORD has been absorbed entirely into sensory perception. The daughters of men, outward appearances, have captured present consciousness. The I AM being presented to Elohim in that moment is "I am a grasshopper." Elohim, who enforces identity after its kind, rules accordingly. The promised land remains unoccupied not because the giants are too strong but because the identity being filed cannot inherit it. The seed of the promise cannot germinate in ground prepared by defeat.

Caleb and Joshua hold a different I AM. They see the same land and the same inhabitants but assume the identity of those able to take possession of it. Their filing differs, and so does Elohim's eventual verdict. The giants do not need to be destroyed. The I AM needs to shift. When YHVH/LORD moves on from the grasshopper state and presents the governing identity, Elohim is bound to enforce what is filed.

Putting an End to the High Things

Paul addresses the same mechanism directly.

Putting an end to reasonings, and every high thing which is lifted up against the knowledge of God, and causing every thought to come under the authority of Christ. 2 Corinthians 10:5

The high things are the Nephilim of inner experience: towering states of identity generated when YHVH/LORD merged with sensory evidence rather than with a chosen and held Ehyeh. The knowledge of God, in the language of the key, is the operational Name, Elohim of Ehyeh, the Judges and Rulers of I AM. Every thought brought under the authority of Christ is every internal voice, the plural government of consciousness, brought into alignment beneath the one governing Ehyeh/I AM. This is the Shepherd gathering the scattered sheep, the fragmented voices of Legion brought under a single fold, the internal government unified beneath the ruling identity. The Ask, Believe, Receive sequence is the corrective: YHVH/LORD recognises the true desire, assumes the Ehyeh that corresponds to it, and Elohim enforces the outcome. The Nephilim dissolve not through battle but through the assumption of a different identity, one that Elohim must now enforce instead.

Names as the Nature of the State

Names in Scripture are identity codes. They reveal the quality of the state being occupied before the narrative unfolds, because Elohim enforces identity after its kind. Nephilim means the fallen ones. The state carries falling in its nature. However great its external stature appears, whatever rises from YHVH/LORD's union with mere sensory evidence carries the quality of descent at its root. Elohim enforces the name.

The causative shade of the word adds a further dimension. The Nephilim do not only embody falling in themselves. They induce falling in whatever consciousness encounters them without a held Ehyeh/I AM to stand on. This is why the spies needed a governing identity before entering the land, and why Caleb and Joshua, holding a different I AM, were not caused to fall by the same giants.

The identity that cleaves to a true Ehyeh/I AM, leaving the familiar state and fully assuming the chosen one, carries a different nature in its name, and Elohim enforces that instead. Israel means he shall prevail. That is the state available when YHVH/LORD leaves the grasshopper I AM behind and presents the governing identity that Elohim is then bound to enforce. The giants do not need to be destroyed. They need to be vacated by the movement of consciousness into a different assumed identity.

The declaration at the burning bush, Ehyeh Asher Ehyeh, establishes the master principle: the governing structure operates in response to the identity assumed as I AM. When YHVH/LORD presents a fallen identity, Elohim enforces its falling. When YHVH/LORD presents the chosen and held state, the Lord God brings it into being. The Nephilim are the Scripture's account of what Elohim produces when the engine runs on the wrong I AM. The rest of the Bible is the record of what becomes possible when it runs on the right one.
